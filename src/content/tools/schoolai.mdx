## Quick answer

SchoolAI is the best solution for schools that want to give students access to AI while maintaining appropriate oversight. Teachers create custom "Spaces" with specific AI personas and guardrails, then monitor every student conversation in real time. If your school is exploring student-facing AI but wants visibility and control, SchoolAI deserves serious attention.

## What is SchoolAI?

SchoolAI provides a managed AI environment where students interact with AI chatbots while teachers maintain full visibility and control. Teachers design custom "Spaces" with specific instructions, boundaries, and learning objectives. Students work within those Spaces; teachers see every conversation as it happens.

That model is different from giving students access to a generic AI chatbot. SchoolAI is built for the classroom, with content guardrails, age-appropriate boundaries, and assignment integration.

## Why teachers and schools care about it

Many schools want to explore student-facing AI but worry about:

- students going off-task or into inappropriate content
- lack of visibility into what students are actually doing
- explaining AI use to families and boards without clear guardrails

SchoolAI addresses those concerns by design. Teachers set the rules. The AI stays on-topic. Administrators and teachers can see engagement patterns, common questions, and learning outcomes. That makes it easier to justify student AI use to stakeholders who are cautious.

## Key features

### Custom AI Spaces

- Teachers design AI interactions with specific instructions and boundaries
- Different Spaces for different assignments, subjects, or learning goals
- Content guardrails keep AI on-topic and age-appropriate

### Real-time monitoring

- See every student conversation as it happens
- Analytics dashboard for engagement, common questions, and learning patterns
- Export and review capabilities for assessment and reflection

### Assignment integration

- Create AI-powered assignments with rubric alignment
- Push to Google Classroom and common LMS platforms
- Track completion and participation

## Where SchoolAI is strongest

SchoolAI is strongest when the question is not just "what helps teachers?" but "what kind of AI experience are we comfortable giving students?"

It is especially useful for:

- schools piloting student-facing AI for the first time
- teachers who want to experiment with AI tutoring or writing support in a controlled way
- districts that need to explain and defend student AI use to boards and families

## Where it is weaker

SchoolAI is not a teacher productivity tool. It does not generate lesson plans, rubrics, or feedback. It is focused on the student-facing experience. Teachers who need workflow support will need other tools like [MagicSchool AI](/tools/magicschool-ai/) or [Brisk Teaching](/tools/brisk-teaching/).

For guided tutoring with stronger instructional framing, [Khanmigo](/tools/khanmigo/) is a useful comparison.
